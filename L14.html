Special Consideration for Children

Children under 6 years are less susceptible to paracetamol toxicity due to:

  • Immature CYP450 enzyme system
  • Rapid glutathione regeneration

Investigations

Laboratory Tests

  • Liver function tests (AST, ALT, bilirubin)
  • Kidney function tests (creatinine, BUN)
  • PT/INR (coagulation profile)
  • Complete Blood Count (CBC)
  • Arterial blood gas (if indicated)
  • Paracetamol blood level

Treatment

Emergency Management

ABC (Airway, Breathing, Circulation) - stabilize the patient

Decontamination

  • Gastric lavage (if presented within 1-2 hours of ingestion)
  • Activated charcoal (if presented within 4 hours of ingestion)

Antidote Therapy

N-acetylcysteine (NAC) is the specific antidote

NAC Administration Protocols

Oral Protocol:

  • Loading dose: 140 mg/kg
  • Maintenance: 70 mg/kg every 4 hours Ã— 17 doses

IV Protocol:

  • 150 mg/kg over 0.5 hours
  • Then 50 mg/kg over 4 hours
  • Then 100 mg/kg over 16 hours

Alternative Treatments

  • Methionine (alternative if NAC not available)
  • Cimetidine (experimental, not standard)

Supportive Care

  • Glucose IV for hypoglycemia
  • Sorbitol (with activated charcoal)
  • Silymarin (milk thistle extract)
  • Hemodialysis (in severe cases)
  • Liver transplant (in fulminant hepatic failure)
