Diabetes Mellitus

Modern concise summary for pediatric diabetes & DKA

Definition

  • Chronic metabolic disorder characterized by hyperglycemia.
  • Caused by insulin deficiency and/or resistance.
  • Leads to abnormal metabolism of carbohydrates, fats and proteins.

Types of Diabetes

  • Type 1: Insulin-dependent (juvenile onset).
  • Type 2: Insulin resistance, usually older age.
  • Monogenic: Single gene defects (rare, may present neonatally).

Type 1 DM – Features

  • Most common endocrine disease in children.
  • Autoimmune β-cell destruction → absolute insulin deficiency.
  • Occurs at 5–7 years and around puberty.
  • Associated with HLA genes.
  • Classic symptoms: polyuria, polydipsia, weight loss, polyphagia.

Diagnosis of Diabetes

  • Fasting glucose ≥ 126 mg/dL.
  • 2-h OGTT ≥ 200 mg/dL.
  • Random glucose ≥ 200 mg/dL with symptoms.
  • HbA1c ≥ 6.5%.

Complications

  • Acute: hypoglycemia, hyperglycemia, DKA, infections.
  • Chronic: retinopathy, nephropathy, neuropathy.
  • Growth & pubertal delay if poorly controlled.
  • Cardiovascular disease and autoimmune disorders.

Treatment of Type 1 DM

  • Basal–bolus insulin regimen (multiple daily injections).
  • Total dose ≈ 0.8–1 U/kg/day.
  • Subcutaneous injections: abdomen, thigh, arm, buttock.
  • Balanced diet & regular exercise.
  • Education of child and parents is essential.

Monitoring

  • Self blood glucose monitoring by glucometer.
  • Targets: fasting 80–120 mg/dL; postprandial 100–140 mg/dL.
  • HbA1c every 3 months (goal ~4.5–6%).
  • Watch for hypoglycemia & injection site problems.

Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)

  • Life‑threatening complication due to insulin deficiency.
  • Characterized by hyperglycemia, ketosis & metabolic acidosis.
  • Often first presentation in children.
  • Precipitated by infection, stress, missed insulin.

DKA: Diagnosis & Management

  • Criteria: glucose >300 mg/dL, ketonemia, pH <7.3 or HCO3 <15.
  • Features: dehydration, vomiting, abdominal pain, Kussmaul breathing.
  • Management: IV fluids, continuous IV insulin (0.1 U/kg/hr).
  • Correct electrolytes (K⁺), monitor closely.
  • Bicarbonate only if pH <7.1.
