Abortion – Summary

Medical Reference Guide

Definition

  • 1 Expulsion of the contents of the gravid uterus before full term
  • 2 Up to 28 weeks → abortion
  • 3 28 weeks to full term → premature birth

Medicolegal Importance (MLI)

  • Claims against doctor performing abortion
  • Claims against someone causing abortion (e.g., quarrel)

Types of Abortion – Spontaneous

  • Occurs without interference (10%)
  • Maternal causes:
    • Infections (syphilis, rubella)
    • Fibroid
    • Retroverted uterus
    • Cervical incompetence
  • Fetal causes:
    • Congenital anomalies
    • Rh incompatibility
    • Congenital infections
    • Poly/oligohydramnios

Types of Abortion – Induced: Therapeutic

  • Performed to save mother's life
  • Requirements:
    • Written consent
    • Qualified doctor
    • Hospital
    • Official notification
  • Indications:
    Maternal
    • DM
    • CHD
    • CRF
    • LCF
    • Preeclampsia
    • Eclampsia
    • Hyperemesis gravidarum
    • Bleeding
    Fetal
    • Congenital anomalies
    • Intrauterine fetal death (IUFD)
    Methods
    • Drugs (PG)
    • D&C
    • Catheter/aspiration
    • C-section (late)

Types of Abortion – Induced: Criminal

  • Without medical indication, to destroy fetus
  • Methods:
    General violence
    • Heavy exercise
    • Lifting
    • Jumping
    • Abdominal blow
    Drugs/Ecobolics
    • Ergometrine
    • PG
    • Pituitary extract
    • Quinine
    • Lead
    Local violence
    • FB in vagina (wood, knitting needle)
    • Antiseptic solutions
    Purgatives
    • Castor oil
    • Croton oil
    • Jalap root
    • MgSO4
    Metals
    • Lead
    • Mercury
    • Arsenic (toxic)

Complications of Abortion

Early
  • Neurogenic shock: parasympathetic (cervical dilatation), sympathetic (pain)
  • Hemorrhage: primary & secondary (infection)
  • Embolism: air, fat, amniotic fluid
  • Acute poisoning
Late
  • DVT (pelvic venous thrombosis)
  • DIC
  • Acute renal failure (shock → ischemia → tubular necrosis)
  • Sepsis & septicemia

Diagnosis of Abortion

In Living
  • Written consent
  • Confirm pregnancy signs
  • General/systemic exam (look for violence, disease)
  • Blood & urine: test for diseases
  • Local exam: cervix (redness, bleeding, edema, lacerations, FB)
  • Product exam: precipitin test
  • Fetal age estimation (length, weight, ossification centers)
In Dead
  • Examine clothes, vaginal contents
  • Autopsy: peritonitis, ruptured uterus, air embolism (RT ventricle, skull)
