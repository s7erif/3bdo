Vaccination in Pediatrics

Preventive Pediatrics & Immunization Schedule

Overview

Vaccination is the most effective preventive strategy in pediatrics. It induces active immunity to protect children from serious infectious diseases and helps achieve herd immunity in the community.

Types of Immunity

  • Innate: inborn, non-specific.
  • Acquired: passive or active.
  • Passive natural: transplacental IgG, colostrum.
  • Passive artificial: immune serum / immunoglobulin.
  • Active natural: after infection.
  • Active artificial: vaccination.

Why Vaccination is Important

  • Protects the vaccinated child.
  • Prevents epidemics.
  • Targets specific diseases.
  • Provides herd immunity.

Types of Vaccines

  • Live attenuated: BCG, OPV (Sabin), MMR, Varicella, Rotavirus.
  • Killed: Pertussis, IPV (Salk), Hepatitis A, Influenza.
  • Toxoid: Diphtheria, Tetanus.
  • Subunit/Polysaccharide: Hib, Pneumococcal, Meningococcal.
  • Recombinant: Hepatitis B, HPV.

Immune Response to Vaccines

  • Primary response: IgM then IgG after first dose.
  • Secondary response: faster, stronger IgG after booster due to memory.

Egyptian Schedule (Simplified)

  • Birth: BCG, OPV, Hep B.
  • 2, 4, 6 months: OPV/IPV, DPT, Hep B ± Hib.
  • 9 months: Measles.
  • 18 months: OPV, DPT booster, MMR.
  • 4–6 years: OPV, DPT booster.

BCG Vaccine

  • Live attenuated bovine TB.
  • Given intradermal, usually at birth.
  • Produces local scar.
  • Complications: lymphadenitis, rare disseminated TB.
  • Contraindicated in immunodeficiency.

Polio Vaccines

  • OPV (Sabin): live oral, induces intestinal immunity, herd effect.
  • IPV (Salk): killed IM, systemic immunity only.
  • OPV contraindicated in immunodeficiency.

DPT Vaccine

  • Diphtheria toxoid + killed pertussis + tetanus toxoid.
  • Primary at 2, 4, 6 months; boosters at 18 months & 4 years.
  • Side effects: fever, local reaction, rare convulsions.
  • Contraindicated in severe reactions to previous dose.

Measles & MMR

  • Live attenuated.
  • Measles at 9 months; MMR at 18 months.
  • Gives long-lasting immunity.
  • Contraindicated in immunodeficiency & severe illness.

Hepatitis B

  • Recombinant vaccine.
  • At birth, then 2, 4, 6 months.
  • IM injection in thigh/deltoid.
  • Minimal side effects.

Hib & Pneumococcal

  • Protect against invasive bacterial infections.
  • Given IM at 2, 4, 6 months with booster.
  • Side effects: local reaction, fever.

Rotavirus & Influenza

  • Rotavirus: live oral at 2, 4, 6 months (before 32 weeks age).
  • Influenza: inactivated IM yearly after 6 months.

Varicella, Hep A, Meningococcal, HPV

  • Varicella: live SC at 1 year and booster.
  • Hep A: killed IM, 2 doses after 1 year.
  • Meningococcal: high-risk children.
  • HPV: adolescents to prevent cervical cancer.

Contraindications

  • Very ill child with high fever.
  • Previous anaphylaxis to vaccine.
  • Live vaccines contraindicated in immunodeficiency.
  • Minor illnesses are NOT contraindications.
