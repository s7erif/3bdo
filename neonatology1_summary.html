Neonatology 1

Modern concise summary of newborn care & assessment

Overview

  • Newborn: first 28 days of life.
  • Term: 37–42 weeks of gestation.
  • Main goal: adaptation to extrauterine life.
  • Focus on respiration, thermoregulation, and homeostasis.

Physiologic Adaptation

  • Initiation of respiration by hypoxia, cold & tactile stimuli.
  • Surfactant prevents alveolar collapse.
  • Thermoregulation: maintain 36.5–37.5°C.
  • Fluid & electrolyte balance (homeostasis).
  • Immature immunity → high infection risk.

Routine Delivery Room Care

  • Provide warmth (warm room, blankets, radiant warmer).
  • Clear airway; suction mouth then nose.
  • Tactile stimulation if not breathing.
  • Umbilical cord clamping & antiseptic care.
  • Infection prevention & eye prophylaxis.

APGAR Score

  • Assessed at 1 and 5 minutes.
  • Parameters: HR, respiration, tone, reflex, color.
  • 7–10: normal; 4–6: moderate depression; 0–3: severe.
  • Persistently low score → risk of brain damage.

Normal Examination

  • Vital signs & anthropometry.
  • Head: moulding, fontanelles.
  • Skin: vernix, peeling, lanugo, Mongolian spots.
  • Heart: soft systolic murmurs may be normal.
  • Meconium within 48h; urine within 24h.

Primitive Reflexes

  • Moro (startle).
  • Sucking & rooting.
  • Grasp reflex.
  • Stepping & placing.
  • Babinski reflex (normal up to 2 years).

Neonatal Resuscitation

  • For babies not breathing or crying at birth.
  • Warm, dry, position, clear airway.
  • Positive pressure ventilation if HR <100/min.
  • Chest compression if HR <60/min.
  • Early ventilation is key to success.

Feeding & Care

  • Early breastfeeding: nutrition, bonding, immunity.
  • If unavailable: formula after clear water.
  • Vitamin K IM to prevent bleeding.
  • Vitamin C from 2nd week.

Classification & High Risk

  • By GA: preterm <37w, term 37–41w, post-term >42w.
  • By weight: LBW <2.5kg, normal, macrosomia >4kg.
  • High risk: prematurity, malformations, low Apgar.
  • Maternal risks: DM, HTN, bleeding, poor care.
