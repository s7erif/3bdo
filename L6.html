Carbon Monoxide Poisoning

Complete Medical Summary

Introduction & Physical Characteristics

  • Carbon monoxide (CO) is a leading cause of poisoning deaths, especially in industrial regions
  • It is colorless, odorless, tasteless, non-irritant
  • It is lighter than air, allowing it to disperse rapidly
  • Often called the "silent killer" due to its undetectable nature

Sources of Carbon Monoxide

Common Sources

  • Produced by cars, trucks, gasoline engines
  • Stoves, lanterns, burning charcoal/wood
  • Gas heating systems
  • Tobacco smoke is a major household source

Indications:

  • COHb >40%
  • Coma
  • Pregnancy >20%
  • Unconsciousness
  • Arrhythmia
  • Ischemia
  • Severe acidosis (pH<7.2)

Mechanism:

Accelerates CO dissociation from Hb and cytochrome oxidase

Investigations & Differential Diagnosis

Investigations

  • CO-oximetry: measures COHb directly
  • Dilution test: COHb blood stays pink on dilution
  • Chest X-ray: ground-glass, edema
  • ECG: tachycardia, arrhythmia, ST/T-wave changes

Differential Diagnosis

  • Alcohol intoxication
  • Angina/MI
  • Psychiatric illness
  • Migraine
