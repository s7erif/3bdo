Introduction to Animal Venoms

Venom is a special category of poison that must be injected by one organism into another one to produce the harmful effects. Usually, the composition of each animal venom is complex.

The venom is usually secreted by special epithelial cells and stored in the lamina of special exocrine glands which may be in the head, tail, or the entire surface of the animal.

Types of Venomous Animals

Snakes

  • Family Elapidae (Middle & Upper Egypt)
  • Family Viperaidae (Desert areas)
  • Family Cortoildae (America)

Scorpions

  • Order Scorpionida
  • Phylum Arachnidesis
  • Nocturnal in nature
  • Seek cool and moist places

Pathophysiology of Envenomation

Frightness Effect

  • Neurogenic shock
  • Cardiac arrest
  • Cerebral stroke triggered by fear

Biological Effect - Local

  • Cytotoxic - Tissue destruction & necrosis
  • Proteolytic - Precipitation of protein
  • Neurotoxic - Motor and sensory effects

Biological Effect - Systemic

  • Cardiotoxic - Myocarditis, arrhythmias, pericarditis
  • Hematotoxic - Coagulopathy, cytotoxic effect on RBCs
  • Allergic - Due to release of Histamine

Scorpion Stings

Scorpions are members of the order Scorpionida and are the most primitive members of the phylum arachnidesis. They are nocturnal in nature and seek cool and moist places. They are not generally aggressive but sting repeatedly when threatened.

Scorpion Venom Composition

Component Description
Low molecular weight proteins Mainly neurotoxic
Enzymes Minimal or no enzymes (Phospholipase A2)
Free amino acids Tryptophan, histamine, serotonin, etc.

Mechanism of Action

Produces repetitive firing of axons by:

  • Maintenance of Na-channels open after depolarization
  • Blocking the voltage-gated K-channels

Pathogenesis of Scorpion Envenomation

Neurological Effects

  • Increased acetylcholine release â†’ Muscarinic syndrome
  • Increased catecholamine release â†’ Hypertension, myocarditis
  • Direct central action â†’ Convulsions & malignant hyperthermia

Cardiovascular Effects

  • Alpha receptors stimulation â†’ Hypertension, tachycardia
  • Myocardial dysfunction
  • Pulmonary edema and cool extremities
  • Excess catecholamines cause accumulation of endothelins and vasoconstriction

Metabolic Effects

  • Suppression of insulin secretion
  • Hyperglycemia, Hyperkalemia
  • Accumulation of Free fatty acids and free radicals
  • Acute Myocarditis

Clinical Picture - Grading System

Grade I

Local pain or paraesthesia at the site of sting

Grade II

Pain or paraesthesia away from the site of sting

Grade III

One or more systemic effects

Grade IV

All manifestations (local & systemic)

Systemic Manifestations

General Manifestations

  • Sweating and cold extremities
  • Thirst, dehydration and fever up to 42Â°C

Neuropsychiatric Manifestations

  • Blurred vision and disturbed eye movements
  • Dysphagia and excessive salivation
  • Tinnitus and slurred speech
  • Involuntary movements and jerky motility
  • Agitation, restlessness, and confusion
  • Convulsions, altered conscious level, or coma
  • Malignant hyperthermia

Cardiovascular Manifestations

  • Tachyarrhythmias & hypertension
  • Heart failure, hypotension, circulatory collapse
  • Cardiomyopathy & toxic myocarditis
  • ECG changes: Depressed S-T segment & inverted T-wave

Other Manifestations

  • Respiratory: Tachypnea, respiratory distress, pulmonary edema
  • GIT: Severe vomiting, diarrhea, hematemesis
  • Genitourinary: Priapism, uterine contractions (may induce abortion)

Poor Prognosis Indicators

  • Altered conscious level
  • Seizures
  • Shock or hypotension
  • ECG changes
  • Pulmonary edema
  • Malignant hyperthermia

Treatment

First Aid Measures

Reassurance of the patient

Immobilization of the affected limb

Application of tourniquet distal to the site of the bite/sting

Avoid: Incision, drainage, suction, ice pack (obsolete methods)

Hospital Management

No life-threatening manifestations:

  • Rapid physical examination and evaluation of vital signs
  • Focused local examination regarding edema and swelling
  • Close observation for 8-12 hours (snakes) or 3-4 hours (scorpions)

Life-threatening manifestations:

  • Emergency: A, B, C (Airway, Breathing, Circulation)

Specific Antidote - Antivenom

Polyvalent anti-venom (venom neutralizing antibodies)

  • Sensitivity test before administration
  • If hypersensitivity reaction occurs: adrenaline IM, hydrocortisone, & antihistaminic
  • Re-administration under close observation if needed

Snake Antivenom Dosing

Envenomation Clinical Presentation Dose (vials)
None No local, no systemic manifestations No antivenom
Minimal Local, no systemic, no lab abnormalities 3-5 vials
Moderate Local beyond the site of bite, mild systemic, mild lab abnormalities 5-10 vials
Severe Severe local, severe systemic, severe lab abnormalities 10-15 vials or more

Scorpion Antivenom Dosing

  • Skin test must be performed before administration
  • Start with one ampoule I.M.
  • If no response within 0.5-1hr, repeat up to 3 ampoules I.M.
  • In severe cases: 1-5 ampoules by slow I.V. drip method

Symptomatic & Supportive Measures

  • I.V. fluid replacement
  • Vasopressors in case of shock (e.g., Dobutamine)
  • Antihypertensive drugs in severe hypertension (Nitroprusside)
  • Fresh blood transfusion in case of coagulopathy
  • Treatment of arrhythmias according to type
  • Pulmonary edema/hypoxia: mechanical ventilation with PEEP
  • Prophylactic antibiotics & antitetanic serum (toxoid)
