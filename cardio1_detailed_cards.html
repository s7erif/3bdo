Cardiology 1

Congestive Heart Failure & Basic Pediatric Cardiology

Introduction

Pediatric cardiology deals with congenital and acquired heart diseases in infants and children. Cardio 1 focuses mainly on congestive heart failure (CHF) and basic concepts of cardiac function.

Definition of Heart Failure

Heart failure is a clinical syndrome in which the heart is unable to pump sufficient blood to meet the metabolic demands of the body, or can do so only at the expense of elevated filling pressures.

Physiology of Cardiac Output

  • Cardiac output = Heart rate × Stroke volume.
  • In infants, CO depends mainly on heart rate.
  • Determinants: preload, afterload, contractility.
  • Any disturbance may lead to heart failure.

Etiology of Heart Failure in Children

  • Volume overload: VSD, PDA, AV canal.
  • Pressure overload: Coarctation, aortic stenosis.
  • Myocardial disease: Myocarditis, cardiomyopathy.
  • Arrhythmias.
  • Systemic causes: anemia, sepsis.

Clinical Features

In Infants

  • Poor feeding and easy fatigability.
  • Excessive sweating, especially during feeding.
  • Tachypnea and respiratory distress.
  • Failure to thrive.

In Older Children

  • Exercise intolerance.
  • Dyspnea and orthopnea.
  • Fatigue.

Physical Signs

  • Tachycardia.
  • Cardiomegaly.
  • Hepatomegaly (most reliable sign in infants).
  • Edema (less common in infants).
  • Basal lung crepitations.

Investigations

  • Chest X-ray: cardiomegaly, pulmonary congestion.
  • ECG: rhythm, chamber enlargement.
  • Echocardiography: key diagnostic tool.
  • Blood tests if systemic cause suspected.

Management Principles

  • Treat underlying cause.
  • Reduce preload and afterload.
  • Improve myocardial contractility.
  • Provide adequate oxygenation and nutrition.

Medical Treatment

  • Diuretics: Furosemide to reduce preload.
  • ACE inhibitors: Captopril to reduce afterload.
  • Inotropes: Digoxin in selected cases.
  • Oxygen therapy.
  • Fluid and salt restriction.

Common CHD Causing CHF

Ventricular Septal Defect (VSD)

  • Most common CHD.
  • Left-to-right shunt → pulmonary overcirculation.
  • Features: CHF, recurrent chest infections.

Patent Ductus Arteriosus (PDA)

  • Continuous machinery murmur.
  • Causes volume overload of lungs and LV.

Prognosis

  • Depends on underlying cause.
  • Good with early diagnosis and proper management.
  • Surgical correction often required for CHD.
