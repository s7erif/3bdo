Carbon Monoxide Poisoning

Complete Medical Summary

Introduction & Physical Characteristics

  • Carbon monoxide (CO) is a leading cause of poisoning deaths, especially in industrial regions
  • It is colorless, odorless, tasteless, non-irritant
  • It is lighter than air, allowing it to disperse rapidly
  • Often called the "silent killer" due to its undetectable nature

Sources of Carbon Monoxide

Common Sources

  • Produced by cars, trucks, gasoline engines
  • Stoves, lanterns, burning charcoal/wood
  • Gas heating systems
  • Tobacco smoke is a major household source

COHb Levels

  • Smokers: 5–6%
  • Nonsmokers: 0.5%
  • Passive smokers: 2–3%

Pathophysiology

1. Hemoglobin Impairment

Hb has 240x greater affinity for CO than O₂ → tissue hypoxia

2. Myoglobin Impairment

CO competes with O₂ → myocardial ischemia, hypotension

3. Mitochondrial Impairment

CO binds cytochrome oxidase → blocks oxidative phosphorylation

Risk Factors & Mode of Poisoning

Risk Factors

  • High altitude
  • Increased ventilation
  • Children
  • Anemia
  • Heart or brain disease

Mode of Poisoning

  • Mostly accidental (closed garages, gas leaks, enclosed spaces in winter)
  • Rarely suicidal

Fatal Dose

  • 0.01% CO in air → 20% COHb
  • 0.1% CO → 50% COHb
  • 1% CO → 70% COHb

Classic Postmortem Finding

Cherry-red skin & mucous membranes

Complications

1. Neuropsychiatric

Memory loss, ataxia, parkinsonism, depression

2. Cardiovascular

Myocardial necrosis, angina, arrhythmia, accelerated atherosclerosis

3. Pulmonary

Aspiration pneumonia, dyspnea

4. Ophthalmic

Retinal hemorrhage, ↓ light sensitivity

5. Dermatological

Bullae, sweat gland necrosis, alopecia

6. Gastrointestinal

Vomiting, diarrhea, bleeding

7. Miscellaneous

Rhabdomyolysis

Treatment

Immediate Treatment

  • Remove from exposure
  • Secure airway
  • Give 100% O₂ (10–12 L/min) until COHb <15–20%

Supportive Care

  • IV fluids, vasopressors
  • Correct acidosis
  • Mannitol/steroids for brain edema
  • Urine alkalinization

Hyperbaric Oxygen (HBO)

Best in first 6 hrs, prevents delayed neurological complications

Indications:

  • COHb >40%
  • Coma
  • Pregnancy >20%
  • Unconsciousness
  • Arrhythmia
  • Ischemia
  • Severe acidosis (pH<7.2)

Mechanism:

Accelerates CO dissociation from Hb and cytochrome oxidase

Investigations & Differential Diagnosis

Investigations

  • CO-oximetry: measures COHb directly
  • Dilution test: COHb blood stays pink on dilution
  • Chest X-ray: ground-glass, edema
  • ECG: tachycardia, arrhythmia, ST/T-wave changes

Differential Diagnosis

  • Alcohol intoxication
  • Angina/MI
  • Psychiatric illness
  • Migraine
