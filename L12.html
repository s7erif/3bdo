Alcohol Toxicity Summary

Comprehensive guide to acute and chronic ethanol toxicity

Acute Ethanol Toxicity

Sources

Alcoholic beverages, household preparations, medications.

Absorption

80% from small intestine, 20% from mouth & stomach.

Metabolism

80-90% in the liver, 10-20% excreted unchanged by kidney & lung.

Fatal Dose & Period

Fatal dose: 150 ml pure alcohol or 400-800 ml diluted.

Fatal period: 1–10 hours (death due to central asphyxia).

Factors Affecting Toxicity

  • Tolerance
  • Fasting
  • Sex (higher in females)
  • Age
  • Liver disease
  • Smoking

Clinical Picture

Mild (150 mg/dl)

  • Euphoria
  • Behavioral disturbance
  • Incoordination
  • Vomiting
  • Alcoholic smell
  • Flush

Moderate (150–300 mg/dl)

  • Hypotension
  • Weak pulse
  • Shallow respiration
  • Cold clammy skin
  • Shock
  • Coma
  • Convulsions

Severe (>300 mg/dl)

  • Coma
  • Respiratory failure
  • Convulsions
  • McEwen's pupil (constricted pupil dilating on cheek pinch)

Chronic Ethanol Toxicity

General Effects

  • Recurrent infections
  • Anorexia
  • Weight loss
  • Red nose

GIT Effects

  • Inflammation
  • Cirrhosis
  • Peptic ulcer

CNS Effects

  • Peripheral neuritis
  • Tremors

Mental Effects

  • Dipsomania
  • Dementia
  • Delusions & hallucinations

Complications

  • Lactic acidosis
  • Teratogenicity

Special Syndromes

Wernicke's Encephalopathy

Due to thiamine deficiency

  • Mental disturbances
  • Ataxia
  • Diplopia
  • Nystagmus

Korsakoff Psychosis

Amnesia (not reversible with thiamine)

Treatment Approaches

Acute Toxicity Treatment

  • ABC (Airway, Breathing, Circulation)
  • Gastric lavage with airway protection
  • No specific antidote
  • Haemodialysis in severe cases
  • Supportive therapy (fluids, NaHCO3 for acidosis, folic acid, warming)

Chronic Toxicity Treatment

  • Psychological & social therapy
  • Multivitamins (thiamine)
  • IV benzodiazepines for withdrawal
  • Disulfiram for long-term recovery (contraindicated in acute intoxication)

Withdrawal Syndrome

Minor (6–8h)

  • Tremors
  • Anxiety
  • Sweating
  • Nausea
  • Sleep disturbance

Moderate (24–36h)

  • Hallucinations (visual, auditory, olfactory)
  • Fever
  • Seizures

Severe (48–72h)

  • Delirium tremens
  • Agitation
  • Disorientation
  • Fever
  • ↑ Heart Rate
  • ↑ Blood Pressure
  • ↑ Respiratory Rate
  • Mortality ~15%
