Child Abuse - Summary

Definition

According to the World Health Organization (WHO), child abuse and maltreatment include all forms of physical, emotional ill-treatment, sexual abuse, neglect, negligent treatment, and exploitation.

These acts cause actual or potential harm to a child's health, survival, development, or dignity, especially when committed by a person in a position of responsibility, trust, or power.

Incidence

  • Age Most cases occur under 5 years, with 25% below 2 years. Sexual abuse includes children below 18 years.
  • Sex Both males and females are equally affected.
  • Patterns Four main types – Physical, Neglect, Emotional, Sexual.

Predisposing Factors

Parental
  • Low education
  • Psychiatric problems
  • Sexual deviations
Child-related
  • Hyperactivity
  • Disability
  • Illegitimacy
Socioeconomic
  • Poverty
  • Poor housing
  • Lack of social support

Types of Child Abuse - Physical Abuse

  • Skin injuries Abrasions, bruises, lacerations (often multiple and of different ages)
  • Head injuries (leading cause of death): Brain contusions, skull fractures, intracranial hemorrhage
  • Eye injuries Retinal/vitreous hemorrhage, lens displacement
  • Visceral injuries (second leading cause of death): Liver, spleen, intestinal trauma
  • Skeletal injuries Fractures in ribs, skull, extremities at different stages of healing
  • Burns Cigarette burns, scalds with spared reflex areas, chemical/electric burns

Types of Child Abuse - Neglect

Failure to provide food, medical care, or basic needs, leading to:

  • Severe neglect Poor development, malnutrition, lethargy
  • Failure to thrive syndrome Growth retardation
  • Developmental delay Poor language, motor, and social skills
  • Medical neglect Untreated physical illness or disability

Types of Child Abuse - Emotional Abuse

Hardest to define but causes serious psychological disturbances in children.

Emotional abuse can include constant criticism, threats, rejection, and withholding love, support, or guidance.

Types of Child Abuse - Sexual Abuse

  • Incest Sexual acts with relatives who cannot legally marry
  • Child pornography Involving children in sexual media
  • Child prostitution Sexual exploitation with multiple partners
  • Child molestation death Murder of children due to sexual motives, usually by males

Special Condition: Shaken Baby Syndrome

  • Mechanism Violent shaking (whiplash effect) affecting immature skull and brain
  • Causes of death Subdural hemorrhage, subarachnoid hemorrhage, retinal bleeding

Management of Child Abuse

  • Medical treatment of injuries
  • Temporary removal of the child from unsafe environments
  • Support and rehabilitation for families
  • Social work intervention to protect the child
