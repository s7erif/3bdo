Rape Definition

Sexual intercourse between a male and a female other than her husband, against her consent. Penile penetration (partial or complete) is enough to constitute rape.

Important: Even partial penetration is sufficient to constitute the crime of rape under legal definitions.

Conditions of Valid Consent

Requirements for Valid Consent

  • Female above 18 years of age
  • Sane (not mentally retarded)
  • Conscious, not under anesthesia/coma
  • Not under influence of drugs (alcohol, cannabis, opium)
  • Not under fraud or deception
  • Not under fear, violence, or threat

Examination of Rape Cases - Victim

Consent Requirements

  • For victims >21 years: consent from female herself
  • For victims <21 years: consent from guardian

History Taking

  • Date and time of incident
  • Location of incident
  • Any delay in reporting

Observation

  • Gait and movement patterns
  • Behavior and emotional state
  • Mentality and cognitive state
  • Body build and physical characteristics

General Examination

  • Torn or damaged clothing
  • Semen or blood stains on clothing/body
  • Abrasions or bruises on body
  • Foreign hair or materials on body

Local Examination

  • Vulval edema or swelling
  • Perineal laceration or tearing
  • Hymenal rupture or damage
  • Vaginal bleeding or presence of semen

Hymen Examination

The hymen is a thin membrane partially covering the vaginal orifice. Rupture typically causes spotting of blood.

Hymen Types

  • Crescentic
  • Annular
  • Dentate
  • Fimbriated
  • Imperforated
  • Cribriform
  • Septate

Causes of Rupture

  • Sexual intercourse
  • Physical trauma
  • Pathological conditions

Recent Rupture

  • Tender to touch
  • Bloody appearance
  • Translucent edges

Old Rupture

  • No tenderness
  • Opaque appearance
  • Healed edges

Virginity

A virgin is defined as someone who has not engaged in sexual intercourse.

Virginity Diagnosis

  • Small or underdeveloped breasts
  • Intact hymen
  • Narrow vaginal orifice
  • Intact post commissure

False Virgin

  • Has engaged in sexual intercourse
  • But presents with intact hymen
  • Possible due to hymen elasticity
  • Or non-penetrative sexual activity

Pregnancy Diagnosis

Probable Signs

  • Morning sickness
  • Vomiting
  • Enlarged abdomen
  • Darkened areola

Sure Signs

  • Fetal movement
  • Palpation of fetus
  • Auscultation of fetal heart
  • Sonar confirmation
  • Positive pregnancy test

Examination of the Accused

Consent is required for examination of the accused. Compare history with victim's account.

Age Determination

  • Individuals under 14 years are usually considered incapable of rape
  • Assessment of physical development
  • Evaluation of mental state

Clothing Examination

  • Torn or damaged clothing
  • Blood stains
  • Victim's hair or fibers

Injury Assessment

  • Nail or bite marks on chest/neck/hands
  • Genital injuries
  • Presence of venereal disease

Forensic Evidence

  • Semen comparison with victim
  • DNA fingerprinting crucial for identification
  • Smell of alcohol or other substances

Sexual Perversions (Paraphilias)

Sodomy

Anal intercourse between males.

Bestiality

Intercourse with animals.

Incest

Intercourse between relatives who cannot legally marry.

Lesbianism

Sexual acts between females.

Necrophilia

Intercourse with corpses.

Indecent Assault

Sexual acts or words without penetration between non-married persons.

Exhibitionism

Exposing genitals for sexual arousal.

Sadism

Arousal by inflicting pain on others.

Masochism

Arousal by receiving pain.

Buggery

Anal intercourse between male and female.
