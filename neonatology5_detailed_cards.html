Neonatology 5

Neonatal Sepsis, Asphyxia & Emergency Care

Overview

Neonatology 5 focuses on common neonatal emergencies including neonatal sepsis, birth asphyxia, hypoglycemia, hypothermia, and principles of neonatal resuscitation and intensive care.

Neonatal Sepsis

Systemic infection occurring in the first 28 days of life with high morbidity and mortality.

Types

  • Early-onset: within 72 hours (maternal origin).
  • Late-onset: after 72 hours (hospital/community).

Etiology & Risk Factors

  • Prematurity and low birth weight.
  • Prolonged rupture of membranes.
  • Maternal fever or infection.
  • Unclean delivery practices.
  • Invasive procedures.

Clinical Features of Sepsis

  • Poor feeding, lethargy.
  • Temperature instability.
  • Respiratory distress, apnea.
  • Jaundice.
  • Seizures or shock in severe cases.

Diagnosis of Sepsis

  • Blood culture (gold standard).
  • Complete blood count.
  • CRP and procalcitonin.
  • CSF analysis if meningitis suspected.
  • Chest X-ray if respiratory signs.

Management of Sepsis

  • Start empirical IV antibiotics immediately.
  • Common regimen: ampicillin + gentamicin.
  • Supportive care: oxygen, fluids.
  • Thermal and glucose control.
  • Modify antibiotics based on culture.

Birth Asphyxia

Failure to initiate and sustain breathing at birth leading to hypoxia and acidosis.

  • Risk factors: prolonged labor, cord prolapse, placental abruption.
  • Low Apgar score at 1 and 5 minutes.
  • May lead to hypoxic-ischemic encephalopathy (HIE).

Clinical Features of HIE

  • Altered consciousness.
  • Abnormal tone and reflexes.
  • Seizures.
  • Poor feeding.
  • Respiratory failure.

Neonatal Resuscitation

  • Warm, dry, and position airway.
  • Clear secretions if needed.
  • Start ventilation if HR <100/min.
  • Chest compressions if HR <60/min.
  • Medications: epinephrine if required.

Neonatal Hypoglycemia

  • Common in preterm, SGA, IDM.
  • Symptoms: jitteriness, apnea, seizures.
  • Treat with early feeding or IV dextrose.
  • Maintain glucose >45 mg/dL.

Neonatal Hypothermia

  • Temperature <36.5°C.
  • Causes: prematurity, cold environment.
  • Complications: hypoglycemia, acidosis.
  • Prevention: warm chain, skin-to-skin care.

Prevention & Prognosis

  • Aseptic delivery and hand hygiene.
  • Early breastfeeding.
  • Timely recognition and referral.
  • Outcome depends on severity and early treatment.
