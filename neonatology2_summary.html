Prematurity, growth disorders & neonatal emergencies

Overview

  • Focus on premature infants and neonatal emergencies.
  • Includes SGA, LGA, IDM, metabolic problems and asphyxia.
  • Early recognition and management improve survival.

Preterm Infant

  • Born before 37 weeks gestation.
  • Most common cause of neonatal mortality.
  • Causes: PROM, preeclampsia, multiple pregnancy, maternal disease.
  • Low birth weight, large head, lanugo, soft ears, hypotonia.

Physiologic Problems in Preterm

  • Poor temperature control → hypothermia.
  • Immature lungs → RDS & apnea.
  • Weak suck & swallow → feeding difficulty.
  • Immature liver → jaundice.
  • Low immunity → sepsis.

Complications of Prematurity

  • Respiratory: RDS, apnea, BPD.
  • Neurologic: intracranial hemorrhage, PVL.
  • GIT: NEC.
  • Hematologic: anemia.
  • Metabolic: hypoglycemia, hypocalcemia, hypothermia.
  • ROP (retinopathy of prematurity).

Management of Preterm

  • Gentle handling, radiant warmer.
  • Incubator: temp & humidity control.
  • Early respiratory support if needed.
  • Parenteral → enteral feeding.
  • Breast milk preferred.
  • Vitamin & iron supplementation.

SGA & LGA

  • SGA: <10th percentile for GA (IUGR).
  • Risks: asphyxia, hypoglycemia, hypothermia, polycythemia.
  • LGA: >90th percentile.
  • Often due to maternal diabetes → birth injuries.

Infant of Diabetic Mother (IDM)

  • Fetal hyperinsulinism due to maternal hyperglycemia.
  • Macrosomia, birth trauma.
  • Risks: hypoglycemia, hypocalcemia, polycythemia.
  • Cardiomyopathy & RDS may occur.
  • Management: early feeding & glucose monitoring.

Metabolic Disturbances

  • Hypoglycemia: glucose ≤40 mg/dl → jitteriness, seizures.
  • Treat: feeding, IV 10% dextrose.
  • Hypocalcemia: Ca <7 mg/dl → seizures, apnea.
  • Treat: IV calcium gluconate.
  • Hypothermia: T <35.5°C → gradual warming.
  • Hyperthermia: T >38°C → cool environment & fluids.

Asphyxia & HIE

  • Failure to establish breathing at birth.
  • Leads to hypoxic-ischemic encephalopathy (HIE).
  • Manifestations: altered consciousness, seizures.
  • Management: resuscitation, supportive NICU care.
  • Prevention is crucial.
