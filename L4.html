Hydrocarbons (Kerosene) Toxicity

Forensic Medicine and Toxicology

By: Dr. Mostafa Asem
Lecturer of forensic medicine and toxicology

Introduction to Kerosene

  • Kerosene is a volatile hydrocarbon
  • Source: Plant in origin, or from distillation of crude petroleum
  • Commonly used as fuel for heating, lighting, and cooking
  • Accidental ingestion is common in children

Mechanism of Action

Local Effects

Irritation of lung & intestine

General Effects

  • CNS depression
  • Sensitize myocardium to circulating catecholamine

Effects of Kerosene Aspiration

  • Hyperemia, irritation, and inflammation in the oropharynx
  • Bronchospasm, chemical pneumonitis
  • Inhibit surfactant â†’ alveolar collapse â†’ hypoxia

Mode of Poisoning

  • Mode of poisoning: Accidentally, suicidal
  • Fatal dose: >1 ml/kg, few drops in aspiration or inhalation
  • Fatal period: 24 h

Cause of Death

  • Early: inhibition of respiratory center, or heart failure & ventricular fibrillation
  • Late: pneumonia

Clinical Picture

GIT Symptoms

Nausea, Vomiting, Diarrhea (smell of kerosene in the vomitus)

CNS Symptoms

Drowsiness, stupor, & coma. Central respiratory failure

Lung Complications

Chemical pneumonitis:

  • Bronchopneumonia with fever
  • Tachypnea, Dyspnea, chest pain
  • Pulmonary oedema
  • Pneumothorax, pneumatocele, pleural effusion

Heart Complications

Ventricular Fibrillation (VF)

Investigations

Laboratory Tests

ABG, electrolytes, ECG, renal & liver functions

Radiology

Chest X-ray showing:

  • Unilateral or bilateral basal infiltration
  • Punctuate perihilar densities
  • Pneumonitis, Pneumothorax, pneumatocele, pleural effusion

Treatment

Emergency Management

A, B, C (Airway, Breathing, Circulation)

Decontamination

  • Skin: remove clothes; wash the skin with water & soap
  • GIT:
    • Emesis: contraindication
    • Gastric lavage: contraindicated except in cases of co-ingestion using cuffed endotracheal tube

Antidote

No specific antidote

Symptomatic Treatment

  • Antibiotics for pneumonia
  • Corticosteroids for chemical pneumonitis
