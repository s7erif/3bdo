Pregnancy and Delivery Summary

Medical and medicolegal aspects of pregnancy and delivery

Medicolegal Importance (MLI) of Pregnancy

  • Divorce To increase alimony
  • Widow Inheritance rights
  • Death Sentence Execution postponed until 2 years after delivery
  • Rape Cases investigation
  • Abortion Criminal abortion investigation
  • Illegal Pregnancy Related cases

Gestational Period

Normal: 280 days = 40 weeks = 10 lunar months

Egyptian Law:

  • Shortest: 6 months
  • Longest: 12 months
  • Child born <6 months or >12 months → paternity can be denied

Diagnosis of Pregnancy – Probable Signs

  • Breast: dark areola, Montgomery tubercles
  • Abdomen: linea nigra, striae gravidarum
  • Vulva/Vagina: dark coloration, secretions
  • Cervix: secretions

Diagnosis of Pregnancy – Sure Signs

  • Clinical (from 4th month): fetal movements, parts, fundal height, fetal heart sounds
  • Radiology: Ultrasound (safe), X-ray obsolete
  • Laboratory: HCG positive in urine (2 weeks after missed period)
  • Postmortem: enlarged uterus, fetal age estimation, microscopic exam if early

Delivery – Medicolegal Importance

  • Inheritance disputes
  • Child exchange
  • Infanticide
  • Disputed paternity

Signs of Delivery (in living)

  • General: exhaustion, pallor
  • Breast: firm, full, dark areola, colostrum
  • Abdomen: lax, flabby, striae albicans
  • Uterus: Immediately after → above umbilicus; 1 week → midway; 10 days → inside pelvis; 6 weeks → normal
  • Vulva/Vagina: perineal tears, lochial discharge (red → yellow → white → gone by 15 days)
  • Cervix: soft, wide → closes gradually in 2 weeks
  • HCG: positive
