Neonatal Respiratory Distress, Apnea & Hematologic Problems

Neonatal Respiratory Distress (RD)

  • Common emergency in newborns.
  • Signs: tachypnea >60/min, retractions, grunting, nasal flaring, cyanosis.
  • Causes: pulmonary, cardiac, CNS, metabolic, airway & chest wall.
  • Assessment: history, exam, ABG, CXR.

Respiratory Distress Syndrome (RDS / HMD)

  • Most common in preterm infants.
  • Due to surfactant deficiency → alveolar collapse.
  • Risk: prematurity, male sex, C/S, diabetic mother.
  • Features: early severe RD, ↓ air entry.
  • CXR: reticulogranular pattern, air bronchograms.

RDS: Management & Prevention

  • Thermoregulation, fluids, correct acidosis.
  • CPAP or mechanical ventilation.
  • Surfactant replacement therapy ↓ mortality.
  • Monitor ABG, SpO₂, electrolytes.
  • Prevention: antenatal corticosteroids (24–34 weeks).

Transient Tachypnea of Newborn (TTN)

  • Most common cause of RD in full-term babies.
  • Due to delayed clearance of lung fluid.
  • Risk: C/S delivery.
  • Mild RD, improves within 24–48 h.
  • CXR: perihilar streaking, fluid in fissures.
  • Treatment: supportive (O₂).

Neonatal Pneumonia

  • Congenital, intrapartum, or postnatal.
  • Organisms: G− (E. coli, Klebsiella), G+ (GBS), CMV, toxoplasma.
  • Risk: PROM, prematurity, chorioamnionitis.
  • Features: RD in first hours.
  • Treatment: broad-spectrum antibiotics.

Meconium Aspiration Syndrome (MAS)

  • Meconium in amniotic fluid aspirated at birth.
  • Risk: post-term, fetal distress, hypoxia.
  • Features: severe RD, hyperinflated chest, meconium staining.
  • CXR: patchy infiltrates, hyperinflation.
  • Management: suction, O₂, ventilation, supportive care.

Neonatal Apnea

  • Cessation of breathing >20 sec or with bradycardia/cyanosis.
  • Common in preterms <28–34 weeks.
  • Causes: prematurity, hypoxia, sepsis, metabolic.
  • Management: stimulation, O₂, caffeine, CPAP if needed.
  • Usually resolves by 37 weeks GA.

Neonatal Cyanosis

  • Blue discoloration due to hypoxemia.
  • Causes: lung disease, CHD, sepsis, CNS, metabolic.
  • Central cyanosis is always abnormal.
  • Needs urgent evaluation & oxygen.

Neonatal Anemia & Bleeding

  • Anemia: due to blood loss, hemolysis, ↓ production.
  • Features: pallor, tachycardia, poor feeding.
  • Treatment: treat cause, transfusion if severe.
  • Bleeding: often due to vit K deficiency.
  • Prevention: vitamin K at birth.

Neonatal Polycythemia

  • Hematocrit ≥65%.
  • Causes: IDM, IUGR, delayed cord clamping.
  • Features: plethora, hypoglycemia, RD, lethargy.
  • Management: hydration, partial exchange if symptomatic.
