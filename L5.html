Asphyxia - Summary

Medical Reference Guide

Asphyxia refers to conditions where there is inadequate oxygen supply to the body tissues.

Definition

Asphyxia is the lack of tissue oxygenation due to failure of the respiratory process.

Types of Asphyxia

  • 1 Pathological: diphtheria, bronchial asthma, tumors of air passages, pontine hemorrhage
  • 2 Toxic: strychnine, oxalic acid, CO, CO2, H2S, morphine, barbiturates
  • 3 Violent: throttling, strangulation, hanging, smothering, choking, traumatic asphyxia, drowning
  • 4 Environmental: low oxygen at high altitude
  • 5 Iatrogenic: medical errors, especially anesthesia

Stages of Asphyxia

Stage 1

Dyspnea & Cyanosis

  • Blue skin
  • High blood pressure
  • ↑ CO2
  • Dyspnea
Stage 2

Coma & Convulsions

  • Petechial hemorrhages (Tardieu spots)
  • Ruptured alveoli (silver spots)
Stage 3

Irregular Respiration & Death

  • Dilated pupils
  • Low BP
  • Cheyne-Stokes respiration

Postmortem Picture

External
  • Cyanosis
  • Hypostasis
  • Bulging eyes/tongue
  • Subconjunctival hemorrhage
  • Bloody froth
Internal
  • Congested organs
  • Dark fluid blood
  • Tardieu spots
  • Silver spots

Smothering

Obstruction of mouth/nose by hand or object.

Types
  • Accidental (overlaying in infants)
  • Suicidal
  • Homicidal
Postmortem Findings
  • Abrasions around mouth/nose
  • Petechiae

Choking

Foreign body obstruction of airways (e.g., seeds, coins, gases).

Cause of Death

Airway obstruction

Postmortem Findings

Foreign body in airways

Traumatic Asphyxia

Compression of chest/abdomen by heavy weight (crowds, collapsed buildings).

Findings
  • Cyanosis
  • Petechiae
  • Rib fractures

Throttling

Compression of neck by hands.

Postmortem Findings
  • Nail abrasions
  • Inward hyoid fracture
  • Hemorrhage in neck muscles

Strangulation

Compression of neck by ligature tightened by hands.

Postmortem Findings
  • Symmetrical transverse ligature mark
  • Inward hyoid fracture

Hanging

Compression of neck by body weight via ligature.

Types
  • Complete
  • Incomplete
  • Typical
  • Atypical
Postmortem Findings
  • Asymmetrical ligature mark
  • Outward hyoid fracture
  • Saliva dribbling
  • Hypostasis in legs

Drowning

Mechanical interference with breathing due to submersion.

Stages
  • Breath-holding
  • Water inhalation
  • Convulsions
  • Death
Types
  • Typical (water in lungs)
  • Atypical (shock/trauma)
  • Secondary (aspiration pneumonia)
Causes of Death
  • Airway obstruction
  • Electrolyte imbalance
  • Cardiac changes
Postmortem Signs - External
  • Froth
  • Washerwoman skin
  • Cutis anserina
  • Glove & stocking appearance
Postmortem Signs - Internal
  • Frothy lungs
  • Rib marks
  • Tardieu spots
  • Sand/mud in stomach
  • Diatoms in tissues
